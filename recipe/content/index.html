Title: How to Make Kimchi Jjigae (김치찌개)

Description: Warm, spicy, and deeply comforting, Kimchi Jjigae is a classic Korean stew made with well-fermented kimchi, tender pork, and savory seasonings. This hearty dish is perfect for chilly days or whenever you crave bold, tangy flavors. Traditionally enjoyed with steamed rice, Kimchi Jjigae highlights the rich umami of aged kimchi, combined with the depth of garlic, gochugaru, and soy sauce.

Dietary Info: Meat Spicy Vegetarian option available

Total Cook Time: ~45 minutes

Servings: 4 people

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups well-fermented kimchi, chopped
  • 200g (7 oz) pork belly or pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ginger, minced (optional)
  • 1 tbsp gochugaru (Korean red chili flakes)
  • 1 tbsp gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 4 cups water or anchovy broth
  • 1 tsp sesame oil
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Optional: tofu, sliced

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the base: In a medium pot, add sesame oil over medium heat. Sauté garlic and onion until fragrant.
  2. Cook the pork: Add pork slices and cook until lightly browned.
  3. Add kimchi: Stir in the kimchi and cook for 5–7 minutes until it softens and releases its juices.
  4. Season and simmer: Add gochugaru, gochujang, soy sauce, and water (or broth). Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20–30 minutes.
  5. Optional additions: Add tofu or extra vegetables in the last 10 minutes of cooking.
  6. Finish: Taste and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Garnish with chopped green onions before serving.
  7. Serve hot: Best enjoyed with steamed white rice.

Source Attribution: Adapted from traditional Korean home-style cooking practices and recipes widely used in Korea.
